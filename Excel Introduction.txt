What is Excel?
- Excel is a spreadsheet application used for organizing, analyzing, and visualizing data. 
- It helps businesses perform calculations, create reports, track performance, and make data-driven decisions.

Key Components of Excel
- Workbook: An Excel file containing one or more worksheets.
- Worksheet: A spreadsheet consisting of rows and columns.
- Cell: The intersection of a row and a column (e.g., A1, B5).
- Formula Bar: Displays and allows editing of cell contents.
- Ribbon: Contains commands and tools organized into tabs.

Importance in Business Analytics
- Excel is widely used to:
- Calculate revenue and profitability.
- Analyze sales performance.
- Track expenses and budgets.
- Create dashboards and reports.
- Perform forecasting and trend analysis.

What is a Formula?
- A formula is an expression used to perform calculations in Excel. Every formula starts with an equal sign (=).

What is a Function?
- A function is a predefined formula that performs a specific calculation.

Tracing Precedents and Dependents

When working with complex spreadsheets, it is important to understand how formulas are linked.

Precedents : Precedent cells provide data to a formula.
Example:=C2*D2
Cells C2 and D2 are precedents because they supply values used in the formula.

Dependents : Dependent cells contain formulas that rely on another cell.
If cell E2 contains:=C2*D2
Then E2 is dependent on C2 and D2.

Why Use Trace Precedents and Dependents?
- Identify source data.
- Detect formula errors.
- Understand worksheet relationships.
- Audit financial and business models.
