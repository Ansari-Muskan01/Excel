                                                                        D-Mart Sales Dataset

Before we start analysing the data, it is important to understand what this dataset represents.

This dataset contains sales transactions from DMart stores across different regions of India.
Every row in the dataset represents a single customer order, and every column contains specific information related to that order.
As Data Analysts, our first responsibility is not to create charts or dashboards. Our first responsibility is to understand the data and the business process behind it.

Understanding the Business Process ,Let's understand how a typical DMart transaction happens:
- A customer visits a DMart store.
- The customer purchases one or more products.
- The order is recorded in the system.
- Information such as product category, quantity, sales amount, discount, and payment mode is stored.
- The order is delivered to the customer.
- The company tracks profit, sales performance, customer trends, and regional performance using this data.
- This entire journey is captured inside our dataset.

Understanding Each Column

Order ID : A unique identifier assigned to each order.
Business Use: Track individual orders , Avoid duplicate transactions, Monitor order history

Order Date : The date when the customer placed the order.
Business Use: Analyze sales trends , Identify peak sales periods ,Compare monthly and yearly performance

Delivery Date : The date when the order was delivered.
Business Use: Measure delivery performance , Calculate delivery time , Identify delays ,Location Information

Region : Represents the geographical area where the order was placed (North,South,East,West)
Business Questions: Which region generates the highest sales?
                    Which region is most profitable?

City : Represents the city where the customer is located.
Business Questions:
Which city contributes the most revenue?
Which city has the highest number of orders?

Category : Represents the type of product purchased.(Electronics,Grocery,Clothing,Home Care,Personal Care)
Business Questions:
Which category sells the most?
Which category generates maximum profit?
Sales Metrics

Sales : Total revenue generated from the order.
Business Question:How much money did the company earn?

Quantity : Number of units sold.
Business Question:How many products were sold?

Discount : Discount offered on the order.
Business Question:
Are discounts helping increase sales?

Profit : Actual earnings after deducting costs.
Business Question:Is the company making money from these sales?

Important Note: High Sales ≠ High Profit(A product can have high sales but low profit because of heavy discounts.)

Customer Information : Customer Type(Represents the category of customer)
Examples: Member ,Regular Customer
Business Questions:
Which customer type generates more sales?
Which customer segment is more profitable?

Payment Mode : Method used by customers to make payments (Cash,Credit Card,Debit Card,UPI)
Business Questions:
Which payment mode is most popular?
How are customer payment preferences changing?

Why Do We Explore Data Before Analysis?
Imagine a doctor giving medicine without understanding the patient's condition.Similarly, if a Data Analyst starts analysis without understanding the dataset, the insights may be incorrect.
Therefore, before analysis we must answer:
- How many records are available?
- Which regions exist?
- Which product categories exist?
- What columns are available?
- Which fields can be used for analysis?
- This process is called Data Exploration or Data Understanding.

Using this dataset, we can answer questions such as:
- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Total Orders
- Average Order Value
- Region-wise Performance
- City-wise Sales
- Category-wise Profit
- Customer Type Analysis
- Payment Mode Analysis
- Delivery Performance
These insights help DMart make better business decisions and improve overall performance.
