What is Data?
- Data is a collection of facts, figures, observations, or information that can be stored and analyzed to make decisions.
- In simple words, any information that can be recorded is called data.

Examples of Data
- Student marks
- Employee salaries
- Product sales
- Customer information
- Attendance records
- Mobile numbers

Why is Data Important?
- Today, every organization generates a huge amount of data.

Companies use data to:
- Understand customer behavior
- Improve business performance
- Increase sales
- Reduce costs
- Make better business decisions
- Without data, companies would have to make decisions based on assumptions rather than facts.

1. Numerical Data : Numerical data consists of numbers and can be used for calculations.
Examples: Age , Salary , Marks , Number of Employees

2. Categorical Data : Categorical data represents labels or categories.
Examples: Gender , Department , City , Product Category

3. Date and Time Data : This type of data represents dates and times.
Examples: 01-Jan-2026 , 15-Feb-2026 , 10:30 AM , 5:45 PM

4. Text Data : Text data contains words, sentences, or descriptions.
Examples: Customer feedback , Product descriptions , Comments , Emails


What is Data Analytics?
- Data Analytics is the process of collecting, cleaning, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data to discover useful information and support decision-making.
- In simple words, Data Analytics helps us understand what happened in the business and why it happened.

Real-Life Example of Data Analytics
- Imagine you own a supermarket.
- You have sales data for the entire year.

You want to answer questions such as:
- Which product sold the most?
- Which month had the highest sales?
- Which city generated the maximum revenue?
- Which category performed the best?
- Finding answers to these questions using data is called Data Analytics.

Data Analytics generally follows five steps.

Step 1: Data Collection (Collect data from various sources)
Examples: Excel files ,Databases , Websites , Surveys , Applications

Step 2: Data Cleaning (Remove errors and improve data quality)
Examples: Removing duplicates , Handling missing values , Correcting incorrect entries

Step 3: Data Analysis (Analyze the data to identify patterns and trends)
Examples: Total sales , Average revenue , Top-performing products

Step 4: Data Visualization (Present findings using charts and graphs)
Examples: Bar Charts , Pie Charts ,Line Charts ,Dashboards

Step 5: Decision Making (Use insights from the analysis to make business decisions)
Example: 
- If a product is selling well, the company may increase its inventory.
- Why Do Companies Use Data Analytics?

Organizations use Data Analytics to:
- Improve sales performance
- Understand customers better
- Optimize business operations
- Reduce costs
- Increase profits
- Support strategic decision-making
- Today, almost every industry uses Data Analytics.
- Examples include: Banking , Healthcare , E-commerce , Education , Retail , Telecommunications

What is Data Science?
- Data Science is an advanced field that combines: Statistics ,Programming ,Machine Learning ,Business Knowledge to extract insights from data and predict future outcomes.
- Data Science not only analyzes historical data but also helps predict future trends.

Real-Life Example of Data Science
- Suppose a company wants to know: "What will next month's sales be?"
- Using historical sales data and machine learning algorithms, Data Scientists can build models to predict future sales.
- This is called Predictive Analytics and is a major part of Data Science.

Difference Between Data Analytics and Data Science

Data Analytics	                                                   Data Science
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Focuses on understanding past data	                   Focuses on predicting future outcomes
Answers "What happened?"	                           Answers "What will happen?"
Creates reports and dashboards	                           Builds predictive models
Uses Excel, SQL, Power BI	                           Uses Python, Machine Learning, AI
Descriptive in nature	                                   Predictive and Prescriptive in nature
Example

Data Analytics: "What were the total sales last month?"
Data Science: "What are the expected sales next month?"


Role of Excel in Data Analytics :
- Excel is one of the most widely used tools for Data Analytics.
- It is often the first tool that analysts learn before moving to advanced tools like SQL, Power BI, Python, and Machine Learning.

What Can We Do in Excel?
- Using Excel, we can:
- Enter and organize data
- Clean and prepare data
- Perform calculations
- Apply formulas and functions
- Sort and filter records
- Create charts and graphs
- Generate reports
- Build dashboards
- Excel provides a strong foundation for a career in Data Analytics.

Conclusion : 
- Data has become one of the most valuable assets in today's digital world.
- Organizations collect and analyze data to make informed decisions, improve performance, and gain a competitive advantage.
- Data Analytics helps us understand the past and present, while Data Science helps us predict the future.
- Excel serves as the first step in this journey and provides the foundation required for advanced analytical tools and technologies.
